# Loops

![Loops homepage screenshot](https://screenshotit.app/loops.so)

## Snapshot

- `Best for`: Startups that want lifecycle email, product updates, and occasional newsletters in one modern tool.
- `Positioning`: Lifecycle email platform with a lightweight, product-friendly feel.
- `Why it stands out`: Strong default fit for signup flows, onboarding sequences, behavior-triggered messaging, and simple broadcasts.

## Assessment

Loops looks like the cleanest default in this set for a small or mid-sized product team. It sits in the sweet spot between newsletter tools that added automations later and heavier customer engagement platforms that assume a more mature data stack.

Its main strength is focus. It is easier to grasp than a larger marketing suite, but it still covers the flows most founders actually need: welcome emails, drip sequences, behavior-based nudges, and one-off sends to selected segments.

## Trade-offs

- Better lifecycle fit than a general email marketing tool.
- Easier to adopt than Customer.io.
- Less depth and enterprise headroom than Customer.io.
- More product-centric than Kit or Brevo.

## Pricing

- `Free tier`: Yes.
- `Typical paid starting point`: around `$49/mo`.
- `Interpretation`: attractive for startups that have outgrown a free plan but are not ready for enterprise-style pricing.

## Verdict

If the use case is “someone signs up, send a welcome series, follow up based on activity, and occasionally broadcast to segments,” Loops is one of the strongest default picks.
